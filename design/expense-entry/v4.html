Expense entry — group-aware & non-group flows

"Add expense" is now global. An expense either belongs to a trip or is a non-group expense (a casual cost with no trip). The question these mockups answer: when you add an expense that isn't already scoped to a trip, how do you pick the people and the (optional) group?

The shared rule across all options: if the expense has no group, it's a non-group expense and shows up only on the Friends tab. If a group is selected, it lands in that trip exactly like today. Launching "Add expense" from inside a trip pre-fills the group to that trip and the people to its members — so the in-trip flow is unchanged. The options below differ only in the global (no pre-set group) entry path.

Option A — People-first

Recommended

Global add starts with "Who's involved?" — pick people (or add by email), then land on the expense form. Matches the casual mental model: "I'm splitting this with these folks." Group defaults to No group; change it on the form if it actually belongs to a trip.

Comparing the options

All three share the same form and the same Group concept. They differ only in the global (no pre-set group) entry path.

OptionGlobal pathStrengthWeakness
A · People-firstRec Pick people → form (group defaults to No group) Matches casual mental model; group stays a deliberate add-on One extra screen vs B for the in-trip... (but in-trip skips it)
B · One form Form with inline Group + With rows Fewest screens; nothing front-loaded Group choice is quiet — easy to mis-file as non-group
C · Destination-first Choose No group / a trip → form Zero ambiguity about where it lands Extra tap on the most common casual case
Why A is recommended: the casual outing is the whole reason this feature exists, and "who am I splitting with" is the first thing you know in that moment. People-first leads with that, keeps the group selector present-but-secondary for the occasional "actually, put this in the trip" case, and — because the in-trip launch pre-fills both group and people — adds zero friction to the existing trip flow. B is the fallback if the extra screen feels heavy; C only earns its extra tap if mis-filing turns out to be a real problem in practice.
