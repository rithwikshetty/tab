Three icon directions to pick from

All use SF Symbols (rendered here with Lucide as a stand-in). Pick A, B, or C.

A — Colour tile recommended

Each category gets its own brand colour from the Sage avatar palette. Memorable at a glance, plays well with the warm bg. The chip strip becomes a row of distinct identity dots.

B — Sage-tinted tile

Mono palette, all tiles tinted with the Sage accent. More cohesive / quieter than A. Less visual identity per category but easier on the eye.

C — Outline, no tile

Stripped back — just the SF Symbol in Sage on the existing white chip. Most minimal, least personality. Same emotional weight as the current emoji.

Honest take read me

at 20px chip size

Packages converge to the same silhouette. The "pizaz" lever isn't the icon library — it's icon size + colour treatment. Make the icon bigger (e.g. 36–44px on expense rows), give it a coloured tile or duotone fill, and SF Symbols looks just as distinctive as Phosphor.

SF Symbols

Apple, free on Apple platforms

Zero setup, native, Dynamic Type free. Calibrated for chrome (tab bars), so when used small they're fine; when used large they read a bit thin. Fix that with a coloured tile behind them.

Phosphor — Duotone

MIT · phosphor-icons/swift

Where it shines is at 32px+ with no tile — the two-tone fill carries personality on its own. If you want icons big on expense rows without coloured tiles, Phosphor is the answer.

Tabler / Lucide

MIT / ISC

Both line-icon families. Cleaner than Phosphor, more "designer-y" than SF Symbols. At 20px they're indistinguishable from SF Symbols.

Bottom line for tab: the chip strip on the New Expense page is small (20–30px). Pick the package on other criteria — dependency tolerance, library coverage for future categories. If you go SF Symbols, lean on the coloured tile (Variant A) for visual identity. If you go Phosphor Duotone, you can probably skip the tile and let the icon carry it — but you add a SwiftPM dependency.
